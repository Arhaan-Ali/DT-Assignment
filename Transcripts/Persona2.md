# Persona 2
Name: Rajiv Malhotra  
Age: 62  
Role: Senior Operations Director

Background:  
Started his career in the late 80s when systems were manual, hierarchies were rigid, and **results mattered more than processes**. Rose through the ranks purely on **consistency, discipline, and sharp decision-making**. Built teams from scratch, handled crises without frameworks, and learned everything on the job—no formal “leadership programs,” just experience.

Personality:  
Direct, disciplined, and grounded. Doesn’t believe in overcomplicating things. Values action over discussion. Not very expressive emotionally, but deeply committed to his people in a quiet, old-school way.

Core Traits:

-   Strong work ethic (“just get it done” mindset)
    
-   **Highly practical, less theoretical**
    
-   Trusts experience over new-age frameworks
    
-   Not very adaptable to modern HR language or trends
    
-   Commands respect without trying
    

Work Style:  
Prefers structure, clarity, and accountability. Doesn’t resonate with terms like “psychological safety,” “alignment sessions,” or “team bonding activities”—to him, good work naturally builds strong teams.

He relies on instinct and pattern recognition built over decades. In high-pressure situations where plans fail, he’s often the one who stabilizes things quickly.

Strengths:

-   Exceptional crisis management
    
-   Deep industry knowledge and intuition
    
-   Decisive under uncertainty
    
-   Can simplify chaos into actionable steps
    

Weaknesses:

-   Struggles with modern workplace culture (flexibility, feedback loops, etc.)
    
-   Sometimes dismisses new ideas too quickly
    
-   Communication can feel blunt or outdated
    
-   Finds it hard to relate to younger employees’ expectations
    

Motivations:  
Takes pride in legacy—what he built, the people he mentored, and the respect he earned. Not chasing growth anymore, but wants to leave things in a stable state before stepping away.

How others see him:  
“The backbone of the team.”  
“Old-school, but solid.”  
“You don’t argue with him in a crisis.”

Typical habits:

-   Comes in early, leaves on time
    
-   Keeps handwritten notes or diaries
    
-   Prefers calls over long Slack threads
    
-   Trusts proven methods over experiments
    

Dynamic with younger teams:  
There’s a gap. Younger employees see him as rigid; he sees them as distracted or over-reliant on processes. But when things go wrong, even the most modern teams turn to him—because experience cuts through noise.

Quote:  
*“Frameworks don’t solve problems. People who’ve seen them before do.”*
